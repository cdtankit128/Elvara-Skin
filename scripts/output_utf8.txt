01  Color Palette Usage
Every professional store follows a structured color palette system. When selecting your brand colors:
•
Use 3 main colors for the entire store:
   →  Primary Color — Main brand color
   →  Secondary Color — Support color
   →  Accent Color — Highlight color for buttons, offers, CTAs
Recommended Color Ratio
60%
Primary Color
Main backgrounds & dominant sections
30%
Secondary Color
Supporting sections & backgrounds
10%
Accent Color
Buttons, highlights & CTA elements
Observe stores carefully and notice how they apply color hierarchy across sections. The goal is to keep the
design clean, balanced, and visually appealing.
02  Study Other Stores Carefully
Before designing your store, analyze successful brands in your niche and observe how they use:
   →  Colors & Spacing
   →  Fonts & Typography
   →  Sections & Product layouts
   →  Trust signals
Your goal is not to copy, but to understand what makes a store look professional and high-converting.
03  Home Page Structure

Your homepage should feel complete and detailed, not short or empty. Make sure to add multiple
sections to create a longer homepage that showcases products, benefits, and brand story.
Required Sections:
Hero Banner with CTA
Featured Collections
Best-Selling Products
Benefits / USP Section
Product Highlights
Customer Testimonials
Trust Badges
Newsletter Signup
A longer, structured homepage improves customer experience and makes the brand look more professional.
04  Using Shopify AI
You are encouraged to use Shopify AI tools to improve your store. Use Shopify AI to:
•
Generate new sections for the homepage
•
Improve product descriptions
•
Optimize layout ideas & generate better copywriting
If you want additional sections not available in the theme, use Shopify AI to help generate them.
05  Fonts & Typography
Choose fonts that match the brand style of your niche:
   →  Fashion brands — Elegant / modern fonts
   →  Tech brands — Clean / minimal fonts
   →  Kids brands — Friendly / playful fonts
Best Practice: Use one font for headings and one for body text. Avoid using too many fonts as it makes the
store look unprofessional.
06  Overall Design Focus
Your primary focus should always be on delivering a store that feels like a real, ready-to-sell brand:
Clean Layout
Strong Branding
Professional Design
Good Visual Hierarchy
Realistic eCommerce Experience
Market-Ready Appearance
